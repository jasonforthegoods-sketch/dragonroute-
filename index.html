Visa & practical travel tips

Everything you need to know before you land — updated regularly as rules change.

Free

144-hour visa-free transit

Citizens of 54 countries including USA, UK, EU nations, Australia and Canada can enter without a visa for up to 144 hours through designated ports: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and others. You must have an onward ticket to a third country.

Standard tourist visa (L visa)

Apply at your nearest Chinese consulate. You need: passport, completed application, photo, round-trip flight confirmation, and hotel bookings. Processing takes 4–5 business days. Single entry is usually sufficient for first-time visitors.

Payment — WeChat Pay & cash

WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate everywhere. Foreign visitors can now link international Visa/Mastercard to WeChat Pay — set this up before you go. Always carry some RMB cash for small vendors, taxis, and rural areas.

Internet & VPN

Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and most Western apps are blocked in China. Download and test a VPN before you arrive — it will not work once you're inside China without prior setup. Local apps to use: Baidu (search), WeChat (everything), Didi (rides).

Best times to visit

Spring (April–May) and Autumn (September–October) are ideal — mild weather, manageable crowds. Avoid Golden Week (Oct 1–7) and Chinese New Year — domestic tourism peaks sharply and prices double.
